05 — UX Guidelines

Rules that build trust

Every pixel is designed for people in high-stress, high-stakes moments. These rules ensure consistency and clarity across every screen.

Color is Semantic, Never Decorative

Color only appears when it communicates risk, status, or action. No colored backgrounds for aesthetics. When a user sees green, amber, or red — they know exactly what it means.

If removing the color loses information, keep it. Otherwise, remove it.

Spacing Rhythm: 4-8-16-24

All spacing uses a 4px base. Elements within a group: 4-8px. Between groups: 16px. Between sections: 24-32px. Consistent rhythm creates the calm, breathable feel of Apple Health.

Every margin and padding must be a multiple of 4px.

Design for Panic

Critical information must be visible without scrolling. Risk level, drug name, and primary action should all be above the fold. Users may be anxious, rushed, or in an ER.

Scan result risk badge visible within 300ms of page load.

Icons: Outlined, 2px, Rounded

Use outlined (stroke) icons, not filled. 2px stroke weight. Rounded line caps and joins. Matches Apple's SF Symbols style — light, precise, consistent.

Lucide React or Heroicons (outline). 20px default, 24px nav.

Cards: White, 16px Radius, Soft Shadow

All cards use white background on #F2F2F7 surface. border-radius: 16px. Shadow: 0 1px 3px rgba(0,0,0,0.04). No heavy borders — shadow alone provides separation.

Cards never have colored backgrounds unless showing risk status.

Every Action, One Tap Away

Scan, View Result, Emergency Card — never more than one tap from any screen. Bottom navigation on mobile. Prominent CTAs. No hunting required.

Scan button always accessible from primary navigation.
